Our Core Programs

"Let us not love with words or speech but with actions and in truth." — 1 John 3:18

Our programs are the vessels through which we deliver God's love to those in the shadows, turning faith into tangible restoration.

Health & Hygiene

The "Agape" Dignity Program

"This is how we know what love is: Jesus Christ laid down his life for us..." (1 John 3:16)

Restoring the self-worth of those who feel forgotten through essential health resources.

Core Project: Esther’s Bucket (Sanitary kits for girls)
Education & Schools

The "Walk in Truth" Program

"Train up a child in the way he should go..." (Proverbs 22:6)

Removing barriers to education, allowing children to walk toward a brighter future with dignity.

Core Project: Sapatu (Shoes & school requirements)
Nutrition & Hunger

The "Daily Bread" Program

"For I was hungry and you gave me something to eat..." (Matthew 25:35)

Directly answering Christ’s call to feed the hungry by providing immediate relief to families in crisis.

Core Project: Food Relief (Urban slum distribution)
WASH & Wellness

The "Living Waters" Program

"But whoever drinks the water I give them will never thirst..." (John 4:14)

Improving community health through sustainable water and sanitation systems that reflect Christ as the source of life.

Potential Project: Community handwashing stations
Economic Empowerment

The "Nehemiah" Sustainability Program

"Let us rise up and build." (Nehemiah 2:18)

Helping families rebuild their financial lives through vocational training and communal investment circles.

Potential Project: Skill-Up workshops
Mentorship & Outreach

The "Great Commission" Program

"Go and make disciples of all nations..." (Matthew 28:19)

The spiritual engine of MAFS, providing mentorship, prayer and the Gospel alongside physical aid.

Potential Project: Hospital visits & youth fellowships

The Heart Behind the Hand

"Tangible Gospel"

At Make a Face Smile, we believe that compassion is the most audible language of the Gospel. Every shoe we provide, every meal we serve and every bucket we deliver is a physical expression of the love of Christ. We don't just serve because there is a need; we serve because we have been first loved by Him.
